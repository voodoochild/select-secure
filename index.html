CryptoCat

Cryptocat is an open source web application intended to allow secure, encrypted online chatting. Cryptocat encrypts chats on the client side, only trusting the server with data that is already encrypted. Cryptocat is offered as an app for Mac OS X or as a browser extension for Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox and Apple Safari.
Tor
Tor is a way to browse anonymously. It routes webpages through relays to obscure what you're doing and looking at. Think of it as asking a friend to take a message and give it to a friend, who gives it to a friend, who gives it to a friend, until it is delivered by someone unrelated to the person who sent it. It's like a game of telephone where the message is preserved intact but it becomes unclear who it was who said it originally.
Use a pseduonym
A pseudonym is a name you use instead of your usual name. It can be a nickname or alias you use regularly (eg js360 is Jon Smith's email address and his username on a variety of websites) or something you use just once and no one will be able to guess is you (there is nothing to connect anon123abc to Jon Smith). The more often you use a pseudonym and the more people and services which know you use it, the less secure it is.
